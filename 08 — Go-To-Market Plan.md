# 08 — Go-To-Market Plan

> Full GTM strategy for FOODX Phase 1 (Ibadan launch) through Phase 2 (Lagos prep). Built around viral seeding, community trust, and ground-level brand presence.

---

## GTM Philosophy

FOODX does not launch with ads. It launches with people.

The product is social by nature — every order is a potential post, every streak is a visible flex, every gift is a shareable moment. The GTM strategy leans into this. The goal is to create a critical mass of active users in a tight geographic area (University of Ibadan + Polytechnic Ibadan corridors) where social proof travels fast and organic word-of-mouth is the cheapest and most effective channel.

**Core launch principle:** Make the first 200 users feel like founding members, not beta testers.

## Pre-Launch (Month 0)

### Restaurant Partnership Outreach

**Goal:** Sign 5–10 restaurants before public launch.

**Outreach approach:**
1. Identify the 15–20 most popular food spots in the UI/Polytechnic corridor
2. Visit in person — the founder pitches face-to-face (no cold emails)
3. Offer: 0% commission for the first 3 months, guaranteed early customer volume, free branded presence on the app
4. Provide each partner with a FOODX restaurant onboarding pack (printed + digital)
5. Set up restaurant dashboards and do a test order together before launch

**Priority restaurant types:**
- Popular spots students already know (Item 7, Chicken Republic)
- Hostel-adjacent local food vendors
- Restaurants with strong Instagram presence (they'll cross-promote)

**Partnership agreement covers:**
- Commission rate (0% for Month 1–3, then 10% standard)
- Menu setup (FOODX team helps build the first menu)
- Photo shoot for menu items (low-cost, high-impact)
- Hours, minimum order, and delivery zone agreement

---

### Rider Recruitment

**Goal:** 5–10 active riders ready for Day 1.

**Recruitment approach:**
1. Tap into existing dispatch rider networks in Ibadan
2. Post in UI students' WhatsApp groups (dispatch riders often in these groups)
3. Offer competitive per-delivery pay: ₦400–₦600 per delivery + tips
4. Require: working phone (Android), valid ID, knowledge of UI/Poly area
5. Onboard with printed rider guide + app walkthrough session
6. First week: founder or team member monitors every delivery manually

**Rider economics (Phase 1):**
- Customer pays: ₦200 delivery fee
- FOODX supplements: ₦200–₦400 per delivery (founder-sponsored in Phase 1)
- Target: sustainable delivery economics by Month 4 (volume-driven)

### Brand Seeding (Pre-Launch)

- Create FOODX Instagram account (`@foodx.ng`)
- Post: 3–5 "coming soon" teaser posts (behind-the-scenes, brand reveal, "something is coming to Ibadan")
- Build a simple waitlist landing page (React, deployed to Vercel)
- Collect phone numbers for early access via a WhatsApp link
- Brief 5–10 social media friends to hype the launch ("did you see this app?")

## Soft Launch: First 200 Users (Month 1)

### Free Delivery Campaign

**Mechanics:**
- First 200 registered users get free delivery on every order for Month 1
- Framed as "Founding 200" — users get a badge on their profile
- Announced via Instagram post + WhatsApp blast + DMs from the founder's network

**Why 200:**
- Manageable volume for a small rider fleet
- Creates genuine scarcity ("only 200 spots") that drives urgency
- Founder-sponsored cost is calculable (200 orders × ₦400 subsidy = ₦80,000 max)

### Free Food Campaign

**Mechanics:**
- 10 users selected randomly each week in Month 1 to receive a completely free meal
- Announced publicly on Instagram: "This week's free food winner: @[username] 🎁"
- Users must order once to be eligible for the following week's draw
- Winner must post a food photo on FOODX to claim

**Why this works:**
- Creates immediate aspiration — anyone could be next
- Drives weekly active orders (users order to stay eligible)
- Generates authentic food photos for the social feed from Day 1

### Celebrity / Influencer Seeding

**Mechanics:**
- Founder leverages personal network to get 3–5 Ibadan-based influencers or campus celebrities to order on launch week
- They post their FOODX order to their WhatsApp and Instagram stories
- FOODX re-shares all UGC on its own account
- No paid sponsorship in Month 1 — this is personal network activation

**Influencer profile:**
- 2,000–20,000 followers on Instagram or TikTok
- Based in Ibadan or University of Ibadan community
- Known in student / tech / food circles

## Channel-by-Channel Plan

### Instagram (@foodx.ng)

**Content strategy:**
- 3–4 posts per week
- Content mix: food photos from partner restaurants (40%), UGC reposts from users (30%), brand/culture posts (20%), promotions (10%)
- Stories: daily — behind the scenes, polls, streak showcases, "friend is eating" moments
- Reels: 2× per week — short, fun, food-forward (15–30 seconds)

**Growth tactics:**
- Comment on food-related posts by Ibadan accounts
- Tag restaurants in their own food photos (they re-share)
- Run a bi-weekly giveaway (free meal) with share + follow mechanic

### WhatsApp

**Strategy:** WhatsApp is the primary social channel in Nigeria for this demographic — not Instagram.

**Tactics:**
- Create FOODX WhatsApp broadcast list (collect numbers via waitlist)
- Send: launch announcement, weekly free food winner, promotions, new restaurant announcements
- Build relationships with hostel rep groups, department WhatsApp groups, and tech community groups
- Encourage users to share their FOODX order screenshots to their own WhatsApp status

**WhatsApp channel** (official): Set up FOODX WhatsApp Channel for announcements (2024 feature, widely adopted in Nigeria).

### University Ambassador Program

**Goal:** 3–5 student ambassadors across UI, Poly, and private universities in Ibadan.

**Ambassador role:**
- Recruit friends to sign up for FOODX
- Host small "FOODX lunch events" (funded by FOODX — order food for 5–10 people)
- Post about FOODX on their personal pages
- Gather feedback from their peers

**Ambassador benefits:**
- Free delivery for the month they're active
- ₦500 credit per successful referral (user must complete first order)
- First access to new features
- FOODX branded merch (cap, hoodie)

**Recruitment:** Via DM on Instagram + WhatsApp to active students with social presence.

### TikTok (@foodxng)

**Strategy:** Aspirational content, not product demos. TikTok is for brand energy, not conversion.

**Content ideas:**
- "POV: Your friend just gifted you food on FOODX 🎁" (lifestyle)
- Streak milestone videos (Day 7, Day 14, Day 30 content from real users)
- Food unboxing (rider arriving, customer reaction)
- Behind the scenes: rider day, restaurant onboarding, team

**Posting cadence:** 2–3× per week. Prioritise organic reach over paid.

## Restaurant Partnership Playbook

### The Pitch (In-Person)

**Who to meet:** Owner or manager, not staff.

**What to bring:**
- Printed 1-page FOODX overview
- A working phone showing the restaurant dashboard demo
- Photos of your brand identity (uniform, app screens)

**The pitch:**
- "We're FOODX — the food delivery app built for students in Ibadan. We're launching in [month]."
- "We're onboarding 5–10 restaurants before we go public. You'd be featured prominently."
- "For the first 3 months: 0% commission. We just want to prove the volume."
- "We'll bring you customers. You cook the food. We handle everything else."

**Objections and responses:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We tried delivery before, didn't work" | "This is different — it's social. Students see their friends ordering from you." |
| "How many users do you have?" | "We're launching with 200 founding users in Month 1. You'd be their go-to choice." |
| "We're too busy" | "Our restaurant app makes it simple. One screen, one tap to accept orders." |
| "What's your cut?" | "Zero for 3 months. Then 10%. Standard for the industry." |

### Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Restaurant profile created in admin panel
- [ ] Menu items added (with photos)
- [ ] Hours confirmed
- [ ] Staff trained on restaurant app (15-minute session)
- [ ] Test order placed successfully
- [ ] Restaurant WhatsApp added to FOODX ops group

## Rider Recruitment Plan

### Job Post (WhatsApp / Facebook Groups)

```
🛵 FOODX Rider — Ibadan

We're a new food delivery app launching in Ibadan.
Looking for reliable dispatch riders who know the UI/Poly area.

Pay: ₦400–₦600 per delivery + tips
Hours: Flexible — you choose when you're online
What you need: Android phone, valid ID, your own bike

DM us on WhatsApp: [number]
```

### Rider Onboarding Session

- 30-minute group or 1-on-1 session
- Cover: how the app works, how to accept orders, what to do if something goes wrong
- Give each rider: printed guide + FOODX-branded phone holder for their bike
